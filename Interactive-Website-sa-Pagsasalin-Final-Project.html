The Aged Mother

ni Matsuo Basho

Long, long ago there lived at the foot of the mountain a poor farmer and his aged, widowed motherThe story centers on the deep bond between a son and his elderly mother — a central value in traditional East Asian culture.. They owned a bit of land which supplied them with food, and they were humble, peaceful, and happy.

Shining was governed by a despotic leaderA ruler who holds absolute and often cruel power over his people. His fear of old age drives the tragic decree of the story. who though a warrior, had a great and cowardly shrinking from anything suggestive of failing health and strength. This caused him to send out a cruel proclamation. The entire province was given strict orders to immediately put to death all aged people. Those were barbarous days, and the custom of abandoning old people"Ubasute" (姥捨) — a legendary Japanese practice of leaving elderly relatives on a mountain to die. Historians debate whether this was actually practiced or purely mythological. to die was not uncommon.

The poor farmer loved his aged mother with tender reverence, and the order filled his heart with sorrow. But no one ever thought twice about obeying the mandate of the governor, so with many deep and hopeless sighs, the youth prepared for what at that time was considered the kindest mode of death.

Just at sundown, when his day's work was ended, he took a quantity of unwhitened riceBrown or unpolished rice — the staple of the poor. Polished white rice was considered a luxury of the wealthy in feudal Japan. which was the principal food for the poor, and he cooked, dried it, and tied it in a square cloth, which he swung in a bundle around his neck along with a gourd filled with cool, sweet water.

Then he lifted his helpless old mother to his back and started on his painful journey up the mountain. The road was long and steep; the narrow road was crossed and re-crossed by many paths made by the hunters and woodcutters. In some place, they lost and confused, but he gave no heed. On he went, climbing blindly upward — ever upward towards the high bare summit of what is known as Obatsuyama"Obasuteyama" (姥捨山) — literally "Mountain of the Discarded Old Women." A real mountain in Nagano Prefecture, Japan, associated with this legend., the mountain of the "abandoning of the aged."

The eyes of the old mother were not so dim but that they noted the reckless hastening from one path to another, and her loving heart grew anxious. Her son did not know the mountain's many paths and his return might be one of danger, so she stretched forth her hand and snapping the twigs from brushesThe mother, even in her final moments, thinks only of her son's safety — secretly leaving a trail of twigs so he can find his way home. This act is the emotional heart of the story. as they passed, she quietly dropped a handful every few steps of the way.

At last the summit was reached. Weary and heart sick, the youth gently released his burden and silently prepared a place of comfort as his last duty to the loved one. Gathering fallen pine needles, he made a soft cushion and tenderly lifted his old mother onto it. He wrapped her padded coat more closely about the stooping shoulders and with tearful eyes and an aching heart he said farewell.

The trembling mother's voice was full of unselfish love as she gave her last injunction. "Let not thine eyes be blinded, my son." She said. "The mountain road is full of dangers. Look carefully and follow the path which holds the piles of twigsThe trail the mother made — her final, selfless gift to her son. In Filipino values, this mirrors the concept of "pagmamahal ng magulang" — a parent's boundless love.. They will guide you to the familiar path farther down."

The son's surprised eyes looked back over the path, then at the poor old, shriveled hands all scratched and soiled by their work of love. His heart broke within and bowing to the ground, he cried aloud: "Oh, Honorable mother, your kindness breaks my heart! I will not leave you. Together we will follow the path of twigs, and together we will die!"

Once more he shouldered his burden and hastened down the path, through the shadows and the moonlight, to the little hut in the valley. Beneath the kitchen floor was a walled closet for food, which was covered and hidden from view. There the son hid his mother, supplying her with everything she needed, continually watching and fearing she would be discovered.

Time passed, and he was beginning to feel safe when again the governor sent forth heralds bearing an unreasonable order. His demand was that his subjects should present him with a rope of ashesAn impossible task meant to test or humiliate the people. The governor's arrogance is contrasted with the mother's wisdom — a classic literary device showing the value of age and experience over brute power..

The entire province trembled with dread. One night, in great distress, the son whispered the news to his hidden mother. "Wait!" she said. "I will think. I will think." On the second day she told him what to do. "Make rope of twisted straw," she said. "Then stretch it upon a row of flat stones and burn it on a windless night." He called the people together and did as she said and when the blaze died down, there upon the stones, with every twist and fiber showing perfectly, lay a rope of ashes.

The governor was pleased at the wit of the youth and praised greatly, but he demanded to know where he had obtained his wisdom. "Alas! Alas!" cried the farmer, "the truth must be told!" and with deep bows he related his story.

The governor listened and then meditated in silence. Finally he lifted his head. "Shining needs more than strength of youth," he said gravely. "Ah, that I should have forgotten the well-known saying, 'with the crown of snow, there cometh wisdom!'A poetic metaphor — white hair (like snow on a mountaintop) symbolizes a life fully lived and the wisdom that comes with age. This is the story's moral." That very hour the cruel law was abolished, and custom drifted into as far a past that only legends remain.

Noong unang panahon, may isang mahirap na magsasaka kasama ang kaniyang matanda at biyudang inaAng kuwento ay nakasentro sa malalim na ugnayan ng anak at ng kanyang matandang ina — isang pangunahing pagpapahalaga sa tradisyonal na kulturang Silangang Asyano. na naninirahan sa paanan ng bundok. Nagmamay-ari sila ng kaunting lupa na nagbibigay sa kanila ng pagkain, at sila'y namumihay ng mapagkumbaba, mapayapa at masaya.

Ang Shining ay pinamumunuan ng isang malupit na pinunoIsang pinuno na humahawak ng ganap at madalas na malupit na kapangyarihan sa kanyang mga mamamayan. Ang kanyang takot sa pagtanda ang dahilan ng trahedya ng kwento., bagaman isang mandirigma, ay may matinding takot at kaduwagan sa anumang bagay na nagpapahiwatig ng paghina ng kalusugan at lakas. Dahil dito, nagpalabas siya ng isang malupit na proklamasyon. Ang buong probinsiya ay binigyan ng mahigpit na utos na agarang pagpapapatay sa lahat ng mga matatandang tao.

Masaklap ang karanasan noong mga panahong iyon, at ang kaugaliang pag-iiwan sa mga matatanda"Ubasute" (姥捨) — isang alamat na kaugalian sa Hapon ng pag-iiwan ng matatandang kamag-anak sa bundok. Pinagtatalunan ng mga mananalaysay kung tunay ba itong isinagawa o mitolohiya lamang. upang mamatay ay hindi na bago. Mahal ng mahirap na magsasaka ang kanyang matandang ina nang may buong-pusong paggalang, kaya naman ang kautusang ito ay nagpuno sa kanyang puso ng pighati.

Nang mag-dapit-hapon na, pagkatapos ng kanyang araw-araw na gawain, kumuha siya ng isang dakot ng hindi pa nilinis na bigasKayumanggi o hindi pa pinakintab na bigas — ang pagkain ng mga dukha. Ang puting bigas ay itinuturing na luho ng mayayaman sa pyudal na Hapon. — ang pangunahing pagkain ng mga dukha — at ito ay niluto, pinatuyo, at ibinuhol sa isang hugis parisukat na tela.

Pagkatapos ay isinakay niya ang kanyang kaawa-awang matandang ina sa kanyang likod at sinimulan ang kanyang mahirap na paglalakbay paakyat ng bundok. Sa wakas ay naabot nila ang tuktok ng bundok na kilala bilang Obatsuyama"Obasuteyama" (姥捨山) — literal na "Bundok ng Mga Iniwang Matandang Babae." Isang tunay na bundok sa Nagano Prefecture, Hapon, na kaugnay ng alamat na ito., ang bundok na timaguriang "pag-abandona sa matatanda."

Ang mga mata ng matandang ina ay hindi pa ganoon kalamlam upang hindi mapansin ang hindi maingat na pagtakbo mula sa isang daan patungo sa isa pa. Kaya't iniunat niya ang kanyang kamay at tahimik na pinipitas ang mga sanga ng mga maliit na halamanAng ina, kahit sa kanyang huling sandali, nag-iisip lamang sa kaligtasan ng kanyang anak — lihim na nag-iiwan ng bakas ng mga sanga upang mahanap niya ang daan pauwi. Ito ang emosyonal na puso ng kwento. habang sila ay dumadaan, at dahan-dahang naghuhulog ng isang dakot tuwing ilang hakbang.

Sa wakas ay naabot na nila ang tuktok. Pagod at puno ng kalungkutan sa puso, maingat na ibinaba ng anak ang kanyang dalahin at tahimik na naghanda ng isang komportableng lugar bilang kanyang huling tungkulin sa kanyang pinakamamahal.

Nanginginig ang tinig ng ina at puno ito ng walang pag-iimbot na pagmamahal habang ibinibigay niya ang kanyang huling habilin. "Huwag mong hayaang mabulag ang iyong mga mata, anak," sabi niya. "Ang daan sa bundok ay puno ng panganib. Sundan mo ang landas na may mga tumpok ng mga sangaAng bakas na ginawa ng ina — ang kanyang huling, walang pag-iimbot na kaloob sa kanyang anak. Sa mga pagpapahalaga ng Filipino, ito ay sumasalamin sa konsepto ng "pagmamahal ng magulang."."

Napatingin ang anak sa dinaanang landas, saka sa kaawa-awang matanda at kulubot na mga kamay ng kanyang ina na puno ng gasgas at dumi dahil sa kanilang ginawa bilang tanda ng pagmamahal. Sinaktan ng matinding damdamin ang kanyang puso at siya'y yumukod sa lupa habang malakas na umiiyak. "O marangal kong ina, ang iyong kabutihan ay tumatagos sa aking puso! Hindi kita iiwan."

Muli niyang pinasan ang kanyang dalahin at dali-daling bumaba sa daan patungo sa maliit nilang kubo sa lambak. Doon itinago ng anak ang kanyang ina, binibigyan siya ng lahat ng kanyang pangangailangan.

Lumipas ang panahon, at muling nagpadala ang gobernador ng mga tagapagbalita na may dalang utos na dalhan siya ng isang lubid na yari sa aboIsang imposibleng gawain upang subukan o mapahiya ang mga tao. Ang pagmamataas ng gobernador ay inihahambing sa karunungan ng ina — isang klasikong pampanitikang pamamaraan na nagpapakita ng halaga ng gulang at karanasan kaysa sa kapangyarihang pisikal..

Isang gabi, ibinulong ng anak ang balita sa kanyang nakatagong ina. "Maghintay ka," sabi nito. "Gumawa ka ng lubid mula sa pinilipit na dayami. Pagkatapos ay iunat mo ito sa ibabaw ng mga patag na bato at sunugin sa gabing walang hangin." Ginawa ng anak ang sinabi at nang tuluyang mamatay ang apoy, nakita nila sa ibabaw ng mga bato ang isang lubid ng maputing abo.

Nasiyahan ang gobernador at nais niyang malaman kung saan nakuha ng binata ang ganoong karunungan. Isinalaysay ng anak ang buong pangyayari. Tahimik na nakinig ang gobernador at sa wakas ay itinaas niya ang kanyang ulo. "Ang Shinano ay nangangailangan ng higit pa sa lakas ng kabataan. Kasabay ng puting korona ng niyebe ay dumarating ang karununganIsang makatulang metapora — ang puting buhok (tulad ng niyebe sa tuktok ng bundok) ay sumasagisag sa isang buhay na ganap na nabuhay at ang karunungang kasama nito. Ito ang aral ng kwento.!" Sa mismong oras na iyon ay pinawalang-bisa ang malupit na batas.
